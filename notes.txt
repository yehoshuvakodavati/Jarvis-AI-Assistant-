- Quantum computers could potentially solve certain problems exponentially faster than classical computers

- Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, timestamp, and transaction data
- Blocks form a chain due to information about previous blocks, making transactions resistant to alteration
- Blockchains are typically managed by a peer-to-peer computer network for use as a public distributed ledger
- Blockchain records are not unalterable but have high Byzantine fault tolerance and exemplify a secure computing system
